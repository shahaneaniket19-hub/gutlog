Bristol Stool Scale — Reference Guide
Understanding your stool type helps identify digestive patterns
Type 1
⬛
Separate lumps
Hard lumps — Severely constipated
Hard, separate lumps like nuts. Difficult to pass. Suggests very slow gut transit, dehydration, or very low fiber intake. Increase water intake and dietary fiber.
Type 2
🌭
Lumpy sausage
Sausage-shaped, lumpy — Constipated
Sausage-shaped but with lumps. Still on the constipated side. May indicate insufficient hydration or fiber. Slow transit time.
Type 3
🥖
Cracked surface
Sausage with cracked surface — Normal
Like a sausage but with cracks on surface. Considered normal. This is a healthy stool for most people.
Type 4 ✓
🍌
Smooth sausage
Smooth, soft sausage — Ideal
Like a sausage or snake, smooth and soft. This is the gold standard. Indicates healthy gut transit time and good hydration.
Type 5
🫧
Soft blobs
Soft blobs with clear edges — Borderline loose
Soft blobs passed easily. Borderline loose. May indicate a slightly overactive gut. Worth tracking which foods trigger this.
Type 6
☁️
Fluffy/mushy
Fluffy pieces, mushy — Mild diarrhea
Fluffy, mushy, with ragged edges. Mild diarrhea. Often related to stress, diet triggers, or IBS flare-up. Common with the gastrocolic reflex overreaction.
Type 7
💧
Watery
Entirely liquid — Diarrhea
No solid pieces at all. Diarrhea. May indicate infection, food intolerance, or severe IBS episode. If persistent, consult a doctor.
